Background vs. Foreground Images

Why does it matter?

Visually they both render the same, but they are used for two completely different purposes.

Background images are implemented with CSS, and as such serve purely aesthetic purposes. Functionally they could be removed without affecting the content on the page as they are decorative.

Foreground images on the other hand are implemented with HTML using your typical multimedia elements. They represent content, and without them the page would lose some of its meaning.

Sometimes you might see developers abusing background images with products on web shops as most browsers don't allow you to easily save background images by right clicking, but this is bad practice.
